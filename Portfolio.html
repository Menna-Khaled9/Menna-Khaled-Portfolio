Electronics project

Exploring the working principles of the 555 timer IC in astable mode and understand how it can be used to generate continuous pulses for controlling LEDs. The circuit demonstrates how resistors, capacitors, diodes, and variable resistors influence timing, frequency, and duty cycle, allowing control of the LED blinking pattern. Through this project, we aim to gain practical knowledge of timing circuits, learn how to calculate and adjust frequency, and solve challenges such as invisible blinking at high frequencies. Furthermore, the project highlights how this simple circuit can be extended to real-world applications such as driving motors, relays, or integrating sensors and counters.
